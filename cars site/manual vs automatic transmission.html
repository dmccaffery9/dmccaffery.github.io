Automatic vs. Manual Transmission

The manual transmission came out in the late 19th century, and that was the only type of transmission that they had, there were no such things as automatic transmissions. A manual transmission car, unlike an automatic car, contains a clutch and a stick lever which allow the driver to change gears freely. Lots of motor enthusiasts like manuals more because they offer a more traditional driving experience, however they aren't fun in some situations (like traffic) and some people are just lazy in todays world, which is why only 6% of cars contain a manual, making them rare. However, the ability to drive a manual may come in handy someday, and is a skill I feel is important, and something that everyone should learn.

Car: A road vehicle, typically with four wheels, powered by an internal combustion engine and able to carry a varying number of people.
